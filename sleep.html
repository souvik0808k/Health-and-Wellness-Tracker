Caffeine

Caffeine delays sleep onset, reduces sleep quality, and can lead to fragmented sleep, especially when consumed close to bedtime, ultimately disrupting the natural sleep cycle and potentially impacting overall health.

Blue light

Blue light from screens suppresses melatonin production, leading to difficulty falling asleep and reduced sleep quality.

Sleeping in
a dark room

Sleeping in a dark room helps increase sleep quality by promoting the production of melatonin, regulating the body's internal clock, and enhancing the depth and restfulness of sleep cycles.

Exercise

Exercise promotes relaxation, regulates sleep-wake cycles, and increases melatonin production, leading to deeper and more restful sleep. Timing exercise earlier in the day is advised for optimal sleep quality.

Meditation

Meditation induces relaxation, reduces stress, and enhances sleep quality by calming the mind and promoting mindfulness, often resulting in improved sleep patterns and deeper rest.
