Vrikshasana

Vrikshasana (Tree Pose) improves balance, strengthens legs/core, and enhances concentration. It also stretches thighs/chest, reduces stress, and promotes mental clarity.

Paschimottanasana

Paschimottanasana (Seated Forward Bend) stretches the spine, hamstrings, and lower back, promoting relaxation and calming the mind while relieving stress and anxiety.

Triangle Pose

Triangle Pose, or Trikonasana, strengthens legs, stretches hamstrings, hips, and spine, improves balance, and stimulates abdominal organs, promoting digestion and relieving stress.

Jumping-jacks

Jumping jacks are a dynamic cardio exercise that engage multiple muscle groups, improve cardiovascular health, and increase overall endurance. They are also effective for burning calories and can be easily incorporated into various workout routines.

Tuck-Toe

Tuck-Toe Up Dog is a variation of Upward-Facing Dog where the toes are tucked under. It strengthens the arms, shoulders, and back while opening the chest and stretching the front of the body.

Front Fold

Front Fold, also known as Uttanasana, involves folding forward from the hips while standing. It stretches the hamstrings, calves, and back, calms the mind, and relieves stress.

Bhujangasana

Bhujangasana, also known as Cobra Pose, strengthens the spine, shoulders, and arms while stretching the chest and abdomen. It improves flexibility, posture, and digestion, and can also help relieve stress and fatigue.

Low Lunge

Low Lunge, or Anjaneyasana, stretches the hip flexors, thighs, and groin, while also strengthening the legs and core muscles. It improves flexibility, balance, and stability.
